About Us

What is a Makerspace or a Hackerspace?

A hackerspace, also known as a makerspace, is a collaborative workspace where people with common interests, often in computers, technology, science, digital art, and DIY projects, come together to share resources, knowledge, and equipment. These community-operated spaces are founded on principles of learning, exploring, and creating in a hands-on, informal environment.

The hackerspace movement is rooted in the ethos of the open-source community, emphasizing peer learning, knowledge sharing, and a "do-it-yourself" (DIY) approach. It empowers individuals to innovate, experiment, and create, transcending traditional academic or industrial boundaries. Hackerspaces serve not just as places for project development, but also as platforms for social interaction, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the promotion of science and technology education in an accessible, community-driven setting.
